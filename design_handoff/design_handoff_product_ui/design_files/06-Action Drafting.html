Statement · 25 Apr 2026 · For attribution to T.S. Kalyanaraman

On the practice of hallmarking — at Kalyan, since 2007.

A statement framed as continuity of practice, not response to controversy. Set for Kerala outlets ahead of the English-press cycle.

For nineteen years, every karat of gold sold under our name in Kerala has passed through a four-stage hallmarking audit, the last of which is conducted at our Thrissur facility — twelve kilometres from the showroom this story names¹.

We welcome the conversation Ms. Pillai has begun. We invite her, and any reader of this paper who has questions, to visit the Thrissur audit floor — unannounced, with their own scales, on any working day. We have been opening this floor to journalists since 2014.

Hallmarking is not a controversy. It is a practice. It is what separates our trade from a market. The BIS circular of February 2024² formalised what regional chains in Kerala — ours among the first — had been doing for a decade.

To the customer who has trusted us for a wedding, an inheritance, or a first piece of gold for a daughter: every certificate we issue is verifiable, in person and online, against the BIS register⁴. If the certificate ever does not match the assay, we will replace the piece — and we will write the cheque from the Chairman's office. That has been our written policy since 2007.

— T.S. Kalyanaraman, Chairman · For release at 10:00 IST
